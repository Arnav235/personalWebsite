ABOUT ME

Hello! I am Arnav Paruthi

I'm a 17-year-old from Toronto, interested in technology and committed to improving people’s quality of life. I’ve made multiple projects with AI and interned at fleetops.ai during the summer of 2019. Recently, I worked with Kidogo, a company that runs early childhood development centres in Nairobi’s slums, to develop a strategy to decrease the ratio of children to caretakers without compromising profitability. I believe that access to basic needs has the largest impact on people’s lives, and I want to ensure everyone across the world has them.

I’ve always been interested in technology. As a child, my interests jumped between iPhones, drones, graphene, and Tesla. At age 14, I started learning to code. I’d tried it before and was discouraged by its difficulty, but after doing a course on Codecademy everything started to make sense. 

While watching coding videos, I came across this new concept called AI. Recognizing faces was cool, but I was blown away by the fact that these algorithms could beat world-class human players at Atari games and Go (an ancient Chinese board game, considered the hardest classical game). I started building my own AI projects and learning about the field. My projects landed me a summer internship at fleetops.ai on the engineering team.

Late 2019, after completing an AI project, I thought about what I wanted to do next. While considering my options, I realized that everything I’d done so far wasn’t real. Nobody was actually using it, it wasn’t impacting people, and what’s the point of building something that doesn’t have a purpose. 

So I started researching potential problems I could help solve through technology. Recently I worked with Kidogo, a company that runs early childhood development centres in Nairobi’s slums, to develop a strategy to decrease the ratio of children to caretakers without compromising profitability. Currently, I’m working with a team to figure out how hermetic bags (an improved on-farm grain storage technology which significantly reduces food loss) could be better distributed in developing countries. 

The things which have the most impact on people’s lives aren’t the rockets going to mars or food delivery to your door (although, that is increasingly important in these times). It’s access to clean water, food, sanitation, shelter, education, fulfilling work, and great personal relationships (I love this Ted talk). I want to make sure everyone has those basic needs.

What I've been up to

Kidogo Challenge

Date:05-04-20 | Posted By Arnav Paruthi

Kidogo is a non-profit which runs early childhood development (ECD) centers in Nairobi's slums. ECD is essential because the foundation of a child’s social, cognitive, and emotional development is laid during their early years. We worked to reduce the ratio of children to staff while maintaining profitability. Kidogo liked our recommendation, and we're working to implement it now!

Identifying Problems in Industry

Date:10-12-19 | Posted By Arnav Paruthi

Over the last couple of months, I've been working to learn about different industries and identify specific challenges I could help solve. So far I've looked into the banking, legal, and insurance industries. It's fascinating to learn how these industries work. They've mastered many things, but their processes have room for growth.

Fleetops Internship

Date:30-08-19 | Posted By Arnav Paruthi

Fleetops is a startup building a platform to match shippers with truck drivers using AI. This means drivers find loads more quickly and have a larger percentage of their truck full, while shippers get a better price. I built data pipelines using apache beam, and a recommendation system suggesting truckloads to drivers. This was an awesome experience and I learnt so much!

Wealthsimple Challenge

Date:01-04-19 | Posted By Arnav Paruthi

I worked with Wealthsimple to solve one of their biggest problems - how to have more Canadian students take advantage of the Canada Learning Bond. My partner and I suggested implementing a referral program with a double-sided reward in Wealthsimple's RESP product. I learnt a lot about RESP's, finance, and referral programs.

Walmart Challenge

Date:07-12-18 | Posted By Arnav Paruthi

My partner and I had the incredible opportunity of presenting our recommendation at Walmart's head office. Our solution was to use a concept in behavioural psychology called "nudging", to encourage customers to maintain cleanly shelves, reducing the amount of time employees would have to spend zoning.
